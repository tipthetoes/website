Identity V: Call of the Abyss

COA 1: Pirates

A novel Orpheus wrote before he'd gotten amnesia.

Somewhere in the world, hidden treasures await. A group of pirates cross the seas in search of this so-called treasure.

COA 2: Steam Capital

Somewhere within the world, a city exists forever experiencing an encroaching frost.

Powered by steam, this city resists the biting cold through a torch.

The Evil Eye can save the city, but it will plunge the entire city into madness.

Of Optical Mirror, Sound Wave, Sands of Time, and Evil Eye Host, Evil Eye Host is the traitor of the group. He chose to betray the group as opposed to let the city freeze to death.

Steam Teen pretends to release the eye, while Evil Eye Host releases the real eye.

The city is saved... (for now)

COA 3: Lovecraft

In a mysteriious art museum, a wealth of wax statues reside.

Vile Blossom becomes Netherwalker's muse as he perfects his masterpiece.

As the others arrive to stop the Abyss's spread, they find Netherwalker already its pawn.

They fight Netherwalker and the Abyss, but Vile Blossom has embraced the Abyss and infects the Puppeteer.

COA 4: The Great Race

Three giant corporations symbolized by the previous COAs hold a never-ending race.

Molten Hound, a creation of the pharmaceutical giant, became a monster after winning the race.

Those who are devoured by him begin to age rapidly. Unless they win.

Fueling Agent, Highway Cavalier, Pumping Tires, and Reflective Mirror fight against the Molten Hound.

In the end, only Highway Cavalier emerges the same as when he entered.

COA 5: Metropolis

The Metropolis is a city of endless feasting, controlled by the one and only Eternal Aurora.

As people give in to the debauchery, they lose themselves, becoming one with the metropolis.

Neon Vanguard, Tabloid, Pirate Radio, Auditorium, and Paperboy refuse this fate.

They start a media war against the Eternal Aurora, hoping to bring the truth to light.

COA 6: The Whisperer in the Darkness

Ecosphere, Heavy Bomber, Remote Signal, and Space Mining are actors shooting a play set in space directed by The Binary Star.

As they follow their script, the actors begin hearing whispers, and the story feels too real.

Before long, they find themselves forced into their roles, absorbed into the Binary Star's story.

COA 7: Fungal Apocalypse

Civilization has collapsed because of an infectious fungal virus.

Doomsday Rescuer, Mycology, Pioneer Research, Rare Case, and Emergency Transport are sent to investigate the fungi.

Of the group, only Rare Case, Pioneer Research's lover, has been infected.

As the group descend into the cave, they are overcome with fungi, merging into one grand performance.

COA 8: Mechanical Wasteland

"Genesis" wakes up and finds himself in a vast, empty wasteland of grass.

He joins Aviatorus, Compasso, Congregator, and Lignarius as they ride Arachnidopter.

They explore the barren wasteland and fight corrosion as their flesh and blood are replaced by wood.

In the end, their companionship becomes their undoing.

COA 9: Eternal Paradise

In a city of paradise, where statues are banned and "winged ones" are seen as holy...

Caged Fire, Deep Blue, Pale Crown, Untamed Song, and "Homeward Path" seek to expose the truth of this false paradise.

Feathers have sprouted over all of them, though it is seen as holy.

As the god Eternal Paradise is paraded through the city, they prepare to rebel.

When the time comes, "Homeward Path" spots someone long forgotten in the crowd.

It convinces her to abandon their plan as they are caught, and Eternal Paradise reveals the truth behind this "paradise."
